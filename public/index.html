Recursive Rewards
Simply Explained

We reward people who either solve or make real contributions. Our first degree peers may not always have the "right person" to refer to us. Instead of badgering them with large referral bonuses, which are no match for the force of existing friendship or altruism, we can save the financial incentives for the peers of and their peers' peers contacts, in order to get directed to promising talent communities.

MIT Media Lab
Experiment Applied

Recursive rewards were offered to chains of successful peer referrals in a nationwide sourcing challenge by the MIT Media Lab team. They recruited nearly 5000 participants in under 9 hours, which was three times more successful than any other team. They were the only ones to apply recursive incentives.
